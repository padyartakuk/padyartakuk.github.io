About

A living revival of takuk/rhyton, between vessel, sculpture, Persian heritage and collectible contemporary design.

Padyar takuk

Padyar takuk is a contemporary Persian metalwork practice founded by Hadi Hafezi in Isfahan, Iran. The name Padyar carries the meaning of guardian or protector; Padyar takuk is the guardian of takuk/rhyton, preserving and renewing this ancient vessel language through the hand of a contemporary maker.

پادیار تکوک

پادیار تکوک، تکوک/ریتون ایرانی را از مسیر فلزکاری معاصر، یکپارچه، بی‌درز و کاملاً دستی احیا می‌کند. این آثار کپی تاریخی یا شیء عتیقه نیستند؛ بلکه آفرینش‌های معاصر هستند که از حافظه تصویری ایران باستان الهام می‌گیرند.

Artist-Maker

Hadi Hafezi combines drawing, traditional metal forming, pitch work, engraving, sculptural relief and finishing in one continuous artistic process.

Hadi Hafezi

Hadi Hafezi is an Iranian artist-maker and metalworker based in Isfahan. His practice is rooted in direct hand experience, traditional master-apprentice learning, self-made tools and a personal commitment to reviving one of the most noble vessel forms of ancient Iranian visual memory.

Not only craft, not only sculpture

In Padyar takuk, design, volume, symbolic animal form, thickness, engraving, surface, function and final signature must remain connected. The integrity of the work depends on a single mind and hand guiding the process from the first sketch to the finished object.

Process

From a flat sheet of metal to a living symbolic vessel-form.

01

Study & drawing

Each work begins with concept, symbolic animal form and hand-drawn studies.

02

Single sheet

The work begins from a sheet of metal, not from assembled ready-made parts.

03

Hand forming

Through repeated hammering and annealing, the body slowly rises into form.

04

Pitch work

The form is supported with pitch for engraving, relief and surface development.

05

Engraving

Feathers, muscles, eyes, texture and symbolic details are created by hand.

06

Finishing

The final surface is cleaned, controlled, polished, signed and documented.

Contemporary Artwork — Not an Antiquity

All Padyar takuk works are contemporary artworks created by Hadi Hafezi in Isfahan, Iran. They are not antiquities, not archaeological objects and not historical replicas. Each work is inspired by ancient Iranian visual memory and traditional Persian metalworking, while remaining an original contemporary creation.

Commissions

Private commissions are accepted selectively, according to concept, scale, metal, symbolic form and technical feasibility.

Bespoke takuk/rhyton

A commission may begin from a symbolic animal, a cultural concept, a chosen metal, a special scale or a collector’s personal narrative. Each commission requires hand-drawn studies, technical review and individual quotation.

Private Studio Visit

The Padyar takuk workshop in Isfahan is available for private visits by prior arrangement, including an introduction to takuk/rhyton history, metal forming, engraving and selected works.
