Contemporary Persian Takuk / Rhyton

Padyar Takuk

Seamless one-piece hand-shaped metalwork by Hadi Hafezi in Isfahan, Iran.

Padyar Takuk revives takuk, the Persian word for rhyton, as contemporary Persian collectible metalwork shaped entirely by hand from a single sheet of metal, without molds, welding, seams, assembled parts, or modern machinery.

Studio Statement

Each work is presented as a new contemporary artwork inspired by ancient Persian forms, not as an antiquity, archaeological object, or historical replica.

The Studio

A contemporary Persian metalwork studio dedicated to the revival of takuk.

Hadi Hafezi

Founded by artist-maker Hadi Hafezi in Isfahan, Padyar Takuk develops rare contemporary collectible works through a fully handmade, seamless metalworking process. Each piece begins with concept, symbolic animal form, hand-drawn studies, and direct shaping of metal through patient manual work.

The Making Process

One sheet. No mold. No welding. No assembled parts.

The studio’s central language is the transformation of a single sheet of metal into sculptural vessel-form through fully manual, seamless shaping.

Single Sheet

Each piece begins from one sheet of metal, preserving the integrity and continuity of the form.

Hand-Shaped

The work is raised, shaped, refined, and detailed through direct hand process rather than casting or machining.

Seamless Body

The absence of welding, molds, seams, or assembled parts is central to the identity of Padyar Takuk.

Authenticity & Documentation

Clearly contemporary. Carefully documented. Never presented as antiquity.

Important Positioning

Padyar Takuk works are contemporary artworks inspired by Persian takuk/rhyton forms. They are not antiquities, not archaeological objects, and not historical replicas. Each work should be accompanied by a clear certificate, process images, material details, artist statement, and professional documentation.
