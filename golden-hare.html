01 · Curatorial Note

Alertness, delicacy and contained motion

Golden Hare / Queen’s Cup brings the language of Padyar Takuk into a quieter and more intimate register. The hare is not presented as decoration; it becomes a vessel-body of alertness, delicacy and compressed movement.

The work holds a different kind of force from the bull or horse forms. Its power is not monumental. It is concentrated in tension, proportion, lightness and the poised stillness of an animal ready to move.

Within the Padyar Takuk virtual gallery, Golden Hare functions as a close encounter: a work where the animal-vessel idea becomes smaller in scale but sharper in sensitivity.

When held in the hand, the work reveals an intimate bodily scale — almost ring-like in its closeness to the fingers, while remaining a contemporary takuk/rhyton artwork rather than jewellery, ornament or accessory.

Form

Hare-form animal-vessel body: alertness, delicacy and vessel memory held in one compact presence.

Scale

A hand-scale encounter: close to the body and fingers, yet still read as an artwork and animal-vessel body.

Practice

Part of Hadi Hafezi’s Isfahan-based practice in hand-shaped metal and contemporary Persian takuk/rhyton form.

02 · Viewing Room

A quiet encounter with the work

The viewing room keeps the hare-form work in a gallery-like context, allowing the image to carry silhouette, intimacy, surface and proportion without turning the page into a commercial listing.

03 · Scale & Technical Language

Small scale, high control

A smaller animal-vessel form does not reduce the discipline of making. It intensifies it.

In an intimate-scale takuk/rhyton, every change in contour, pressure, surface and proportion becomes more visible. The work must hold the animal presence and the vessel idea without becoming ornamental, decorative or merely descriptive.

Its closeness to the hand gives the work a bodily intimacy: it can be encountered near the fingers like a small sculptural presence, without becoming a wearable object.

Golden Hare is a study in alert stillness: a small body of metal carrying animal sensitivity and vessel memory.

04 · فارسی

خرگوش زرین / جام ملکه

خرگوش زرین / جام ملکه، زبان پادیار تکوک را در مقیاسی آرام‌تر و نزدیک‌تر نشان می‌دهد؛ جایی که خرگوش فقط نقش تزئینی ندارد، بلکه به بدنی جانور-ظرف با حس بیداری، ظرافت و حرکت فشرده تبدیل می‌شود.

نیروی این اثر از جنس شکوه بزرگ‌مقیاس نیست. نیروی آن در سکوت، تناسب، حساسیت سطح، و حالتی است که گویی جانور در لحظه‌ای پیش از حرکت ایستاده است.

هنگامی که در دست گرفته می‌شود، خرگوش زرین مقیاسی نزدیک به بدن انسان پیدا می‌کند؛ حضوری ظریف و تقریباً انگشترگونه در نزدیکی انگشتان، بی‌آنکه به زیور، اکسسوری یا شیء صرفاً تزئینی تقلیل پیدا کند.

در این اثر، مقیاس کوچک‌تر به معنی اهمیت کمتر نیست؛ بلکه کنترل بیشتری می‌طلبد. هر خم، هر سطح و هر نسبت باید همزمان حضور جانور و حافظه ظرف را حفظ کند.

این اثر عتیقه، شیء باستان‌شناسی، کپی تاریخی یا بازسازی موزه‌ای نیست. اثری معاصر از زبان پادیار تکوک است؛ زبانی که در آن فلز، جانور، ظرف و دست در یک پیکره واحد به هم می‌رسند.

05 · Documentation

Authenticity and review context

Padyar Takuk works are presented through contemporary-art language and careful documentation. When prepared for private professional review, documentation may include an artwork sheet, certificate of authenticity, not-antiquity statement and process evidence where documented.

The essential clarification remains constant: this is a newly created contemporary artwork by Hadi Hafezi in Isfahan, not an antiquity, archaeological object or historical replica.
