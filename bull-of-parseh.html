The Bull of Parseh · Contemporary Persian Takuk / Rhyton
01 · Curatorial Note

Animal, vessel and architectural memory

The Bull of Parseh is one of the clearest works for understanding the visual language of Padyar Takuk. The bull is not treated as ornament; it becomes the structural and symbolic presence of the vessel itself.

The work draws from the ceremonial memory of Parseh — known globally through Persepolis — where architecture, animal form, column capitals and imperial visual language meet in a disciplined sense of presence. In this contemporary takuk/rhyton, that memory is not copied. It is re-entered through hand-shaped metal, proportion, silence and animal force.

The result is a work that stands between sculpture and vessel: a contemporary animal-vessel body carrying the gravity of the bull, the discipline of metal and the restrained dignity of Persian architectural memory.

Form

Animal-vessel body: bull, vessel and sculptural presence held within one continuous visual language.

Memory

Inspired by Parseh and the Achaemenid bull-capital imagination, without claiming to reproduce a historical object.

Practice

Part of Hadi Hafezi’s Padyar Takuk practice in Isfahan, focused on hand-shaped metal and symbolic form.

02 · Viewing Room

A quiet encounter with the work

The viewing room keeps the work in a gallery-like context. The image is allowed to carry presence, surface, shadow and proportion without turning the page into a commercial listing.

03 · Technical Language

One body, shaped by hand

In Padyar Takuk, the value of the work is not only in the final image. It is also in the discipline required to keep animal, vessel and sculptural body unified.

The Padyar Takuk practice is defined by seamless one-piece hand-shaped metalwork: the work begins from metal and is shaped through the pressure, patience and control of the hand, without reducing the animal-vessel form to assembled decorative parts.

The object is not a decorative cup. It is a body of metal where animal presence, vessel memory and Persian form become one.

04 · فارسی

تکوک گاو پارسه

تکوک گاو پارسه یکی از روشن‌ترین آثار برای شناخت زبان پادیار تکوک است؛ جایی که گاو فقط نقش تزئینی نیست، بلکه به حضور نمادین و بدنیِ ظرف تبدیل می‌شود.

این اثر از حافظه تشریفاتی پارسه الهام می‌گیرد؛ از جهانی که در آن معماری، سرستون‌های گاو، وقار آیینی و زبان تصویری هخامنشی در کنار هم معنا می‌سازند. اما تکوک گاو پارسه کپی یک شیء تاریخی نیست. این اثر، خوانشی معاصر از آن حضور است؛ خوانشی که از مسیر دست، فلز، تناسب، سکوت و نیروی جانوری شکل می‌گیرد.

در این اثر، ظرف و پیکره از هم جدا نیستند. گاو، بدن ظرف را معنا می‌کند و ظرف، حضور گاو را به یک تکوک معاصر تبدیل می‌کند. نتیجه، اثری است میان مجسمه، ظرف، حافظه معماری ایران و زبان زنده فلزکاری معاصر.

این اثر عتیقه، شیء باستان‌شناسی، کپی تاریخی یا بازسازی موزه‌ای نیست. هدف آن تکرار گذشته نیست؛ بلکه ادامه دادن زبان تکوک/ریتون ایرانی در قالب اثری معاصر، دست‌ساخته و دارای هویت هنری مستقل است.

05 · Documentation

Authenticity and review context

Padyar Takuk works are presented through contemporary-art language and careful documentation. When prepared for private professional review, documentation may include an artwork sheet, certificate of authenticity, not-antiquity statement and process evidence where documented.

The essential clarification remains constant: this is a newly created contemporary artwork by Hadi Hafezi in Isfahan, not an antiquity, archaeological object or historical replica.
